Agritourism

Home / Agritourism

Common models

  • Wedding / event venue: high capex; branding, permitting, and operations intensive.
  • Cabins / weekend rentals: scalable; test demand before major builds.
  • Hunting: seasonal; can pair with habitat work and grazing.
  • Dude ranch experiences: highest labor and liability; depends on staff and experience design.

What tends to drive success

  • Access from major metros, and road quality.
  • Unique “story” and amenities (not just land).
  • Insurance and safety planning.
  • Operations: cleaning, staffing, maintenance, guest experience.
Agritourism is often more “hospitality business” than “land business.” Underwrite it that way.

Economics at a glance

  • Revenue: bookings × price × seasonality.
  • Costs: build capex, insurance, utilities, staffing, marketing, repairs.
  • Risk: demand volatility; reviews; permitting and neighbor constraints.

Low-risk sequencing

  • Start with a small “proof of demand” unit.
  • Validate wedding/event demand before building dedicated venues.
  • Layer with leases (hunting/grazing) to stabilize cashflow.
