Traditional energy leases (oil & gas)

Home / Traditional Energy

Lease basics (high-level)

  • Bonus: upfront payment for signing (structure varies).
  • Royalty: share of production revenue (details depend on deductions/clauses).
  • Primary term: time allowed to start production.
  • Pooling/unitization: how acreage is combined for development (impacts payouts).

Surface vs minerals

  • In Texas, mineral rights may be separate from surface ownership.
  • Know what you own before negotiating.
This page is informational only. Energy leases can be complex; use a Texas oil & gas attorney for review.

Practical next steps

  • Confirm ownership: deeds, title work, and mineral interest documentation.
  • Collect data: prior leases, pooling history, existing easements.
  • Talk to professionals: landman + attorney (and potentially a mineral manager).
  • Clarify surface impacts: roads, pad sites, water use, and remediation language.

Red flags to watch (conceptual)

  • Unclear deductions and post-production cost language.
  • Weak surface protections or vague restoration requirements.
  • Broad assignment rights without notice.
  • Terms that restrict other land uses unnecessarily.
