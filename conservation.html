Conservation & stewardship

Home / Conservation

What conservation can look like

  • Habitat restoration (native grasses, riparian buffers, brush management strategy).
  • Wildlife improvements aligned with hunting and long-term land value.
  • Soil and water conservation practices.
  • Long-horizon programs that prioritize ecological outcomes.

Funding and support (high-level)

  • USDA/NRCS cost-share programs: often require plans and compliance.
  • State/local programs: vary by area and focus.
  • Private and NGO support: sometimes available for targeted habitats/species.
Program eligibility and requirements are specific to land conditions and location. Start by documenting baseline conditions.

How to start (practical checklist)

  • Map your property: water, access, slopes, flood zones, current vegetation.
  • Define goals: habitat, erosion control, water quality, wildlife, aesthetics.
  • Gather docs: ownership, maps, photos, prior management history.
  • Contact local conservation offices for screening and next steps.
  • Plan for maintenance: many “wins” require follow-through.

Pairing strategy

  • Conservation + hunting leases can reinforce each other.
  • Conservation + grazing can work with managed rotation.
  • Conservation can de-risk long-term land value even if cashflow is modest.
