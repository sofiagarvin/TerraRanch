Cattle

Home / Cattle

Two common approaches

  • Owner-operated: higher upside, higher labor and operational risk.
  • Lease / custom grazing: lower labor, more predictable, depends on a good lessee and infrastructure.

Infrastructure reality check

  • Reliable water (ponds, wells, troughs) and seasonal variability.
  • Fencing condition (perimeter and cross-fencing).
  • Access roads, gates, and handling pens if owner-operated.
  • Brush management and pasture health.

Questions to ask a potential lessee

  • Stocking plan and grazing rotation approach.
  • Who handles fence/water repairs and on what timeline.
  • Insurance coverage and liability expectations.
  • Payment timing and what triggers adjustments (drought, feed costs).
This page is informational. Lease terms and liability vary; use local professionals for contracts.

Lease structures (high-level)

  • Per-acre lease: simple, common for pasture.
  • AUM-based: ties payment to grazing capacity.
  • Custom grazing: landowner retains ownership of cattle; operator is paid for management (more complex).

Operational risk factors

  • Drought and water availability.
  • Fence failure and livestock escape liability.
  • Brush encroachment reducing carrying capacity.
  • Market volatility if owner-operated.
