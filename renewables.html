Renewable energy leases

Home / Renewables

What developers typically evaluate

  • Land characteristics: contiguous acreage, slope/terrain, floodplain considerations.
  • Access: roads for construction and long-term maintenance.
  • Interconnection: proximity to transmission/substations (often decisive).
  • Setbacks & neighbors: county rules, visibility, and local sentiment.

Common lease phases (conceptual)

  • Option / development period: developer studies the site; payment is usually lower.
  • Operating period: if built, payments typically increase and persist for years.
Site viability is highly location-specific. Interconnection and permitting can dominate “paper economics.”

Economics drivers (high-level)

  • Project size, layout constraints, and usable acres.
  • Transmission congestion and curtailment risk.
  • Developer cost of capital and power contract terms.
  • Decommissioning obligations and land restoration language.

Landowner risk checklist

  • Clear terms for decommissioning and restoration.
  • Assignment rights (who can take over the lease).
  • Access/easements, fencing, and livestock interaction.
  • Tax impacts and insurance requirements.
